Project Overview

The transition to electrified transportation and energy systems is a pivotal component of California’s strategy to combat climate change. However, the success of this transition depends not only on technological advancements but also on data accessibility, financial viability, and equitable adoption across diverse communities.

Our hypothesis is that by enhancing access to localized data on electric vehicle (EV) adoption, residential electrification, and their correlated impacts on greenhouse gas emissions, we can empower communities to make informed decisions about energy usage and infrastructure investment. Through policy analysis and the development of user-friendly dashboards, we aim to provide clear, actionable insights that highlight cost savings, policy effectiveness, and environmental benefits.

Our work is grounded in the understanding that socioeconomic disparities, lack of awareness, and policy misalignment have hindered the widespread adoption of electrification solutions, and addressing these challenges requires a data-driven, community-centric approach.

Our Focus Areas

To explore this hypothesis, our project focuses on three interconnected themes:

  1. Residential Electrification: We analyze the economic and environmental trade-offs associated with switching from natural gas to electric appliances, such as heat pumps and induction stoves. Our dashboard simulates how factors like electricity rates, home heating efficiency, and local climate conditions influence cost savings and emissions reduction.
  2. EV Adoption: We evaluate the barriers that different communities face in transitioning to electric transportation. By leveraging historical data on EV sales, charging infrastructure availability, and regional income levels, we identify opportunities for increasing adoption.

Our Mission

This integrated approach is critical for ensuring that California’s electrification transition does not deepen existing economic and environmental inequalities. While electrification promises long-term cost savings and climate benefits, access to these benefits remains unevenly distributed.

By providing transparent and actionable data, we seek to mitigate disparities, inform policy, and support communities in making decisions that align with both financial feasibility and sustainability goals. Our work ultimately aims to bridge the gap between policy, technology, and consumer decision-making, ensuring that the transition to a clean energy future is both effective and inclusive.
